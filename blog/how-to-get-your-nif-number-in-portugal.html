Getting Your Portuguese NIF Number: A Step-by-Step Guide for Americans

By the Visa Wise Team · August 5, 2025

If you're planning to move to Portugal, whether on a D7 Passive Income Visa, a D8 Digital Nomad Visa, or any other route, there is one nine-digit number you will need before almost everything else. Your NIF (Número de Identificação Fiscal) is Portugal's tax identification number, and it unlocks the rest of your life in the country. This guide explains what it is, how Americans can get it remotely before arriving, what the in-person process looks like, and why the FATCA complication makes your choice of bank matter.

What Is a NIF and Why Does It Matter?

A NIF is Portugal's equivalent of a US Social Security Number for tax purposes, a unique nine-digit identifier assigned by the Portuguese tax authority (Autoridade Tributária e Aduaneira, operating through local Finanças offices). Almost every financial or legal transaction in Portugal requires it.

You will need your NIF to:

  • Open a Portuguese bank account
  • Sign a rental contract or purchase property
  • Register with AIMA (the immigration authority) once you arrive
  • Set up utilities in your name (electricity, gas, internet)
  • Pay taxes as a Portuguese resident
  • Use the national health service (SNS)
  • Purchase a mobile phone plan under a contract
  • Apply for a Residency Permit (Autorização de Residência)

In short: you cannot build a life in Portugal without a NIF. The good news is that getting one is entirely achievable before you leave the US, which saves you significant time and stress once you land.

Option 1: Remote NIF via Fiscal Representative (Recommended for Most Americans)

This is the route the majority of Americans take, and for good reason. A fiscal representative is a Portuguese-registered individual, typically a lawyer, accountant, or specialist service, who acts as your official representative with the Portuguese tax authority while you are a non-resident. They apply for your NIF on your behalf at a local Finanças office.

What You Need to Provide

  • A clear copy of your valid US passport (photo page)
  • Proof of your current address in the US (utility bill, bank statement, or driver's licence showing address, typically no older than 3 months)
  • Signed authorisation letter appointing the representative (usually provided as a template by the service)

Cost and Timeline

Fiscal representative services typically charge €50–200 for a straightforward NIF application, depending on the service level and provider. Some include the first year of fiscal representation in that fee; others charge an annual renewal fee (usually €80–150/year) to remain your registered address for Portuguese tax purposes while you are still considered a non-resident taxpayer.

Processing time is typically 1–2 weeks from submission of complete documents. The NIF is issued almost immediately once the Finanças appointment is made, the wait is usually in the scheduling of that appointment, not the processing itself.

Recommended Approach

Several English-language services specialise in NIF applications for non-residents, including platforms like Bordr.io, Global Citizen Solutions, and Expatica's NIF service. Verify current availability and pricing directly, as offerings change. Alternatively, any Portuguese immigration lawyer can arrange this as part of a broader visa application service, often the most streamlined option if you're already working with legal counsel on your visa.

Pro tip: Get your NIF sorted at least 4–6 weeks before your VFS appointment. Some steps in the visa application process, particularly proving ties to Portugal such as a rental agreement, go more smoothly when you already have a NIF in hand.

Option 2: In-Person at a Finanças Office After Arriving

If you're already in Portugal, on a tourist visa, visiting before your move, or simply prefer to handle it in person, you can walk into any Finanças (tax office) and apply directly. This is free of charge.

What to Bring

  • Your original passport
  • Proof of Portuguese address (a signed rental contract works well), or documentation of an upcoming AIMA appointment if you don't yet have an address
  • If you don't have a Portuguese address, you may still need to appoint a fiscal representative, the office will advise you on this

The appointment itself is typically quick, the NIF is issued same-day or next-day. Finanças offices can be busy; arriving early or booking an online appointment (where available) saves time. The main practical challenge for first-time visitors is the address requirement: without a rental contract, you may find yourself in a circular situation where you need a NIF to rent an apartment but need an address to get a NIF. This is precisely why getting the remote NIF done before you arrive avoids the headache entirely.

The FATCA Complication for Americans Opening Portuguese Bank Accounts

Here is a complication that catches many Americans off guard, and it's important to know about it before you walk into a Portuguese bank.

FATCA, the Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act, is a US law that requires foreign financial institutions to report accounts held by US citizens or permanent residents to the IRS. From a Portuguese bank's perspective, this means that opening an account for an American client creates significant ongoing compliance obligations: annual reporting, additional documentation, and regulatory risk if the bank fails to comply.

The practical result is that some Portuguese banks are reluctant, and in some cases flat-out refuse, to open accounts for American clients, regardless of your NIF status or residency permit.

Which Banks Are Most Accommodating

Based on consistent applicant experience in 2024–2025, the two banks most reliably open to American clients are:

  • Millennium BCP. Portugal's largest private bank and generally the most internationally experienced with American clients
  • Novo Banco, also generally accommodating, particularly in branches that deal frequently with foreign clients

Success can still vary by branch and individual manager. Having a fiscal representative or immigration lawyer who has relationships with specific branch advisers can meaningfully improve your odds. Some clients report success at other banks (CGD, Santander Portugal), while others report being turned away, there's no guarantee across the board.

Interim Solutions While You Get Sorted

While you navigate the banking process, two digital options can serve as practical interim accounts:

  • Wise, a multi-currency account with an EU IBAN that doesn't require a NIF and accepts US clients. Excellent for receiving US income and making EUR payments.
  • Revolut, similarly accessible, supports EUR accounts, and is widely used among the Portugal expat community. Neither constitutes a full Portuguese bank account, but both are practical for day-to-day transactions while your NIF and main bank account are being established.

Important: Remember that as a US citizen, you remain obligated to file US tax returns and report foreign bank accounts via FBAR and FATCA Form 8938 regardless of where you live. Portugal's tax advantages are real, but US filing obligations follow you. Consult a US-Portugal tax specialist before establishing residency.

Once You Have Your NIF: What to Do With It

The NIF card itself looks like a small laminated slip of paper, keep it somewhere very safe and separate from your passport. More practically:

  • Memorise the number. You'll be asked for it constantly, at the supermarket checkout for loyalty receipts, at every utility setup, at every government office. Having it mentally available saves time.
  • Store a digital photo. Photograph both sides and keep it in a secure password-protected note or cloud storage.
  • Give it to your landlord. Your rental contract will include your NIF; without it, the contract cannot be properly registered with the tax authority.
  • Update your fiscal representative. Once you have a permanent Portuguese address, notify your fiscal representative so they can update your registered address with Finanças. An outdated address can cause problems with tax correspondence.

Getting your NIF is a simple step, but it unlocks everything that follows. Do it early, do it remotely if possible, and treat it as the foundation of your Portuguese administrative life.
